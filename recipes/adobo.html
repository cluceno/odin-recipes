Chicken Adobo

Chicken adobo is a very popular filipino dish. Although, each home makes it slightly different, the core ingredients stay relatively the same. Hope you enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup of can vinegar
  • 6-8 cloves, smashhed and roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp of whole black peppercorns
  • 3-4 dried bay leaves
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1-2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1 teaspon of sugar

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken in a large bowl with soy sauce and half of the garlic for at least 30 minutes or up to 3 hohurs in the fridge.
  2. Sear the chicken and brown the pieces for 3-4 minute sper side until the skin is golden.
  3. Sauté garlic for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Pour in the marinade, water, pepporcorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then immediately lower the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20-25 minutes.
  5. Add vinegar. Important: Do not stir for the first 2 minutes after adding vinegar; letting it cook off creates a smoother, less harsh acidity.
  6. Remove the lid and continue simmering for another 10-15 minutes. This allows the sauce to thicken and the flavors to concentrate. If you prefer a sweeter adobo, stir in the sugar now.
  7. For a traditional finish, simmer until the liquid is mostly reduced and the chhicken starts to fry slightly in its own rendered fat.
  8. Discard the bay leaves and serve hot over plenty of steamed rice.
